The purpose of the website is to analyse team compositions and rider performance in Zwift Ladder racing for the LEQP community.

Data displayed & Sources

The information displayed is obtained from public third-party services (e.g., ZwiftPower and race result platforms). This includes rider names, team affiliation, and performance metrics such as power data and race statistics (including weight-based metrics like W/kg).

We only store data necessary for race analysis. We do not collect or store private contact information, addresses, or identity-specific data beyond public racing profiles. Performance data is used "as-is" to provide community insights.

Cookies & Hosting

Race Matcher

COURSE × RIDER INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM · LEQP LADDER EDITION v1

What is this?

Race Matcher helps your team prepare for ladder races — select the best route, build the optimal lineup, analyse the opponent, and generate a team race strategy powered by AI. Data is fetched from ZwiftPower (last 90 days of power curve data) and combined with actual ladder race results.

Daily use

1. Select your team

Select your active LEQP team from the dropdown at the top of the page. The rider list, captains and team profile update instantly. Switch teams to simulate internal matches.

2. Select riders

Under TEAM ROSTER check the riders available for this match. Use the All checkbox to select/deselect all. Click a rider to expand their full power curve:

  • ● CURVE — real measured data from ZwiftPower (90 days)
  • ○ est. — estimated values based on W/kg and weight

3. Select opponent

Select the opponent team from OPPONENT LIBRARY. Filter by rung first, then select the team. Your own LEQP teams also appear under ── Own Teams ── for internal matches. Uncheck any riders not available for this match.

4. Analyze the matchup

Once an opponent is selected, click 🔍 Matchup in the tab bar. Select a route and number of laps. The matchup page shows:

  • Power Head to Head — line chart comparing average power curves across all key durations (15s · 30s · 1min · 2min · 5min · 20min) in W/kg or Watt view.
  • Power Delta bars — split bar chart showing your advantage or deficit at each interval. W/kg is the default (best for most routes); switch to Watt for flat/sprint routes.
  • Rider Profiles — each rider is classified based on their power curve:
    • SPRINTER — very high 5s peak relative to FTP
    • PUNCHEUR — strong 1-min power relative to FTP
    • CLIMBER — high W/kg and light body weight
    • DIESEL — strong sustained power across all durations
  • Chief Strategist — automatic tactical verdict with route-aware advice based on the selected course type and your team's strengths.
  • Route Analysis — all selected routes ranked from best to worst for your matchup. Use this to argue for or against specific routes.
  • Match Prediction — individual rider-vs-rider scoring for the selected route. Each rider is scored by how well their power profile matches the route's demands. Requires a route to be selected.

5. AI Race Strategy

Click 🤖 AI-Strategi on the Matchup page to generate a tailored race strategy. A route must be selected first. The AI receives full rider data including W/kg across all durations, body weight, rider profile, and — crucially — each rider's average finish position from actual ladder races. This is used to assign a race rating:

  • MATCH WINNER — avg finish #1–2 · highest finishing potential, active role throughout
  • RELIABLE — avg finish #3–4 · solid points scorer
  • VARIABLE — avg finish #5–6 · capable but results vary, used tactically
  • STRUGGLES — avg finish #7+ · sacrifice role candidate

The strategy covers: overall approach (offensive or defensive), individual rider roles with realistic position targets, a lap-by-lap race plan, and a contingency for when the plan breaks down. The AI understands Zwift drafting mechanics, correct attack power profiles, and that this is a team points race — total team score matters more than any single rider winning.

A ⬇ PDF button appears in the strategy box after generation — click it to download the strategy as a printable document.

6. DS Sheet

Click 📋 DS Sheet to open a clean race-day reference sheet in a new tab. Shows W/kg for key durations (15s · 1min · 5min · 20min) for both teams side by side. The highest value per category is highlighted with ★.

7. Download PDF

Click ⬇ Download PDF on the Matchup page to save the full matchup analysis as a PDF — useful for sharing with teammates before a race.

8. Select routes

Under the COURSES tab, select the routes in the race pool. Filter by distance, world, or search by name. Selected routes are used by Race Planner and Matchup.

9. Run the match

Go to ⚡ Race Planner and click ⚡ RUN MATCH. Choose mode:

  • Find best COURSE — ranks all selected routes by strategic advantage vs the opponent
  • Rank RIDERS — shows which riders from your pool best suit a specific route

10. Ladder overview

The 📊 Ladder tab shows all teams ranked by ladder position. Your active team is highlighted. Percentages show how each opponent compares to your team across FTP W/kg, 5min and 1min. Enable ±7 places to filter to teams within challenge range.

11. Rider Intel

The 👤 LEQP Riders tab shows full race history for each LEQP rider — every ladder race with position, power data and weight. Stats include best position, average finish position, and number of races. Average finish position is the most important indicator of actual race ability and is used directly by the AI strategy.

12. Fixtures

The 📅 Fixtures tab shows upcoming scheduled matches with date, time, home/away teams and route.

Understanding the Matchup bars

The split bars work like a tug-of-war centred at zero. A bar growing to the left in red means the opponent leads at that interval. A bar growing to the right in green means you lead. The number is the raw difference — e.g. +0.25 W/kg at 5min means your team averages 0.25 W/kg more at that duration.

W/kg bars are the default and most useful for the majority of routes. Switch to Watt view for flat or sprint-dominated routes.

Understanding Match Results

DimensionWhat it means
TT / FlatLong sustained flat effort — raw watts dominate
SprintShort flat route with bunch sprint finish
PunchRepeated short sharp climbs — 1-min power key
HillyRepeated medium climbs (3–10 min efforts)
ClimbLong sustained climb — W/kg dominates
EnduranceLong route where sustained FTP matters most

The route fingerprint is based on pre-defined vELO weights per route. The dominant type drives which power dimension matters most for scoring and the Chief Strategist verdict.

Laps

Next to the Race Route dropdown, the Laps field scales the route — distance, elevation and fingerprint all update accordingly. The maximum is capped so total distance stays under 40 km.

Route Diagnosis

Click 📊 Route Diagnosis under the logo to open a full table of all routes with fingerprint scores across all five dimensions and the dominant type for each.

Dark / Light mode

Use the ☀ Light / 🌙 Dark button in the top right to switch themes. Your preference is saved automatically.
