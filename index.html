Purpose

The purpose of the website is to analyse team compositions and rider performance in Zwift Ladder racing for the LEQP community.

Data displayed & Sources

The information displayed is obtained from public third-party services (e.g., ZwiftPower and race result platforms). This includes rider names, team affiliation, and performance metrics such as power data and race statistics (including weight-based metrics like W/kg).

Race Matcher

COURSE × RIDER INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM · LEQP LADDER EDITION v1

What is this?

Race Matcher helps your team select the best route before a ladder race — and the best rider lineup for that route. The system fetches rider data from ZwiftPower (last 90 days of power curve data), analyses strengths and weaknesses for both teams, and ranks all available routes by who has the advantage.

Daily use

1. Select your team

Select your active LEQP team from the dropdown at the top of the page. The rider list, captains and team profile update instantly. Switch teams to simulate internal matches.

2. Select riders

Under TEAM ROSTER check the riders available for this match. Use the All checkbox to select/deselect all. Click a rider to expand their full power curve:

  • ● CURVE — real measured data from ZwiftPower (90 days)
  • ○ est. — estimated values based on W/kg and weight

3. Select opponent

Select the opponent team from OPPONENT LIBRARY. Filter by rung first, then select the team. Your own LEQP teams also appear under ── Own Teams ── for internal matches. Uncheck any riders not available for this match.

4. Analyze the matchup

Once an opponent is selected, click 🔍 Matchup in the tab bar. This is the primary race preparation tool and shows:

  • Power Head to Head — a line chart showing average power curves for both teams across all key durations (15s · 30s · 1min · 2min · 5min · 20min) in either Watt or W/kg view. Green = your team, red dashed = opponent.
  • Butterfly Chart — a symmetrical bar chart comparing the two teams side by side. Each bar shows the delta at a given duration — green bars grow right when you lead, red bars grow left when the opponent leads.
  • Pure Watt Delta — average watt difference at every interval. Use this for flat and sprint routes.
  • W/kg Delta — same comparison in watts per kilo. Use this for climbing routes where Zwift's physics are weight-dependent.
  • Rider Profiles — each rider is automatically classified based on their power curve:
    • SPRINTER — very high 5-second peak relative to FTP (sprint/FTP > 2.8)
    • PUNCHEUR — strong 1-minute power relative to FTP (1min/FTP > 1.65)
    • CLIMBER — high W/kg (>4.2) and light body weight (<72 kg)
    • DIESEL — strong sustained power, no obvious peak — thrives on flat and long efforts
  • Chief Strategist — automatic tactical verdict with route-aware advice. The text adapts to the selected course type — a climbing route generates different advice than a flat TT or punchy circuit.
  • Route Analysis — all selected routes sorted from best to worst for your matchup. Use this to argue for or against specific routes in the race pool.

5. DS Sheet

Click 📋 DS Sheet next to the PDF button to open a clean race-day reference sheet in a new tab. The DS Sheet shows only W/kg for the four key durations (15s · 1min · 5min · 20min) for both teams side by side. The highest value in each category on each team is highlighted with ★. The sheet follows your chosen Dark/Light theme.

6. Select routes

Under the COURSES tab, select the routes in the race pool. Filter by distance, world, or search by name. Selected routes are used by both Run Match and Matchup.

7. Run the match

Go to ⚡ Race Planner and click ⚡ RUN MATCH. Choose mode:

  • Find best COURSE — ranks all selected routes by strategic advantage vs the opponent
  • Rank RIDERS — shows which riders from your pool best suit a specific route

8. Ladder overview

The 📊 Ladder tab shows all teams on the selected rung ranked by ladder position. Your active team is highlighted and compared against every other team — percentages show how each opponent's FTP W/kg, 5min W/kg and 1min W/kg compares to yours. Enable ±7 places to see only the teams within challenge range across rungs. The rung auto-selects to match your active team when you open the tab.

Understanding the Matchup bars

The split bars work like a tug-of-war centred at zero. A bar growing to the left in red means the opponent has the advantage at that interval. A bar growing to the right in green means you have the advantage. The number shown is the raw difference — e.g. +25W at 1 min means your team averages 25 watts more at that duration.

Use the W/kg bars for climbing routes and the pure watt bars for flat routes — Zwift uses raw watts on the flat and W/kg on gradients.

Understanding Match Results

DimensionWhat it means
TTLong sustained flat effort — raw watts dominate
SprintShort flat route with bunch sprint finish
PunchRepeated short sharp climbs — 1-min power key
MediumRepeated medium climbs (3–10 min efforts)
ClimberLong sustained climb — W/kg dominates

The score blends raw watts (most important on flat routes) and W/kg (most important on climbs), reflecting Zwift's physics model. Strategic advantage (+/-) is the difference between your team's best lineup and the opponent's best lineup for that specific route.

Laps

Next to the Race Route dropdown on the Matchup tab, the Laps field lets you specify how many times the route is ridden. Short routes (under ~4 km) are typically ridden multiple times in ladder races. Setting laps to 3 scales the route's distance, elevation and punch count — which shifts the fingerprint and scoring accordingly. The maximum is automatically capped so total distance stays under 40 km.

Route Diagnosis

Click 📊 Route Diagnosis under the logo to open a full table of all 169 routes with their calculated fingerprint scores across all five dimensions (TT · Sprint · Punch · Medium · Climb). The DOMINANT column shows which dimension the algorithm scores highest. The DOMINANT column shows which dimension the algorithm scores highest.

Dark / Light mode

Use the ☀ Light / 🌙 Dark button in the top right to switch between the dark Racing theme and the light theme. Your preference is saved automatically.
